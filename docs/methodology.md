# Editorial Methodology

## Content selection

The articles included in *Science & Disinformation Briefs* were selected according to their relevance to current debates about scientific disinformation and their potential interest for journalists and science communicators.

Priority was given to recent scientific publications available through open access sources, ensuring that readers can directly access the original research.

## Scientific sources evaluation

Each selected paper was evaluated considering:

- relevance to the topic of disinformation;
- scientific credibility of the publication venue;
- accessibility of the original source;
- potential impact on public communication.

## Content transformation

Scientific papers were transformed into accessible editorial articles through a process of:

1. identification of the main research question;
2. extraction of the most relevant findings;
3. adaptation of technical concepts into accessible language;
4. verification against the original publication.

The objective was to preserve scientific accuracy while making the content understandable for a broader audience.

## Editorial structure

Each article follows a common structure:

- journalistic introduction;
- explanation of the scientific context;
- summary of research findings;
- link to the original open-access publication;
- social media snippet for possible redistribution.

## Review process

Before publication, each article was manually reviewed to verify:

- accuracy of the scientific information;
- correct attribution of sources;
- clarity of the explanation;
- consistency of the editorial style.
